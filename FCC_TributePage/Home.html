Dr. Verghese Kurien

Father of white Revolution in INDIA

Milkman of India

Dr. Verghese Kurien, a social entrepreneur whose "billion-litre idea", Operation Flood – the world's largest agricultural dairy development programme, made dairy farming India's largest self-sustaining industry and the largest rural employment provider. It made India the world's largest milk producer from a milk-deficient nation, which doubled milk available per person and increased milk output four-fold, in 30 years.

His Journey to become the Milkman of India:

  • 1940 : Attained his degree in B.Sc in Physics from Loyola College in Madras.
  • 1943 : Attained bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering from the College of Engineering, Madras.
  • 1948 : He was chosen to study Dairy engineering under Govt. of India scholarsip and went on to the United States of America but returned with a degree in Master of Science in Mechanical Engineering by choosing some dairy electives.
  • 1949 : He headed for Anand, a place in Kaira district of Gujarat where he was supposed to spend five years as an officer of the Dairy division in return for the scholarship paid by the government.
  • 1950 : He started noticing the movement of the farmers in the village and admired the qualities of their leader, Tribhuvandas Patel. On the insistance of Tribhuvandas Patel, joined the 'Kaira District Cooperative Milk Producers' Union Limited (KDCMPUL)' cooperative as General Manager and helped Tribhuvandas Patel in setting up a new dairy in the village.
  • 1955 : He along with dairy engineer H. M. Dalaya, started experimenting to obtain skim milk powder and condensed milk from abundant buffalo milk instead of cow milk whose supply is short in India thus giving a strong competition to Europe's Nestle.
  • 1957 : 'AMUL' was born which stood as an acronym for Anand Milk Union Limited derived from a sanskrit word 'AMULYA' meaning priceless.
  • 1960 : Later research by Dr. G. H. Wilster led to cheese production from buffalo milk at Amul.
  • 1965 : Prime Minister Lal Bahadur Shastri tasked Kurien to replicate the dairy's 'Anand pattern' nationwide for which, the National Dairy Development Board (NDDB) was founded under Kurien, independent of governmental control.
  • 1970 : His "Billion-litre idea" to stem the movement of high-yield cattle of native breeds to urban areas, which subsequently, would face needless slaughter, reverse this flow by setting up milksheds & dairies all over the nation and stabilise the markets of big cities for their ensuing produce.
  • 1979 : He founded the Institute of Rural Management Anand (IRMA) to groom managers for the cooperatives. Premier Alexei Kosygin invited Kurien to the Soviet Union for advice on the cooperatives there.
  • 1982 : Pakistan invited him to set up dairy cooperatives, where he went leading a World Bank mission.
  • 1989 : China implemented its own Operation-Flood like programme with the help of Kurien and the World Food Programme.
Kurien's support was crucial in making, the 'Amul girl' ad campaign (advertising with a larger public message), one of the longest running for decades now.
